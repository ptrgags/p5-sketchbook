Perspective Railroad (2025)

This animation draws inspiration from both math and art. I recently read Perspective Made Easy by Ernest R. Norling. The book is a handy tutorial on drawing in perspective in traditional art. A couple of constructions were helpful here. First, the method to draw evenly-spaced fence posts is useful for spacing the bottom of each railroad tie. I also used the method to divide a perspective rectangle into even sub-divisions. This was useful for giving the railroad ties thickness.

I've also been exploring Geometric Algebra, and found that 2D Plane-based Geometric Algebra (PGA) is perfect for perspective drawings like this. It allows you to "join" two points into a line, or "meet" two lines to find the point of intersection. This is much like drawing with a straightedge on paper.
