Seashell Texture (2021)

This animation is a simulation of the pigmentation patterns on seashells, based on the 1992 SIGGRAPH paper, Modeling Seashells by Fowler, Meinhardt and Prusinkiewicz.

In particular, this triangle-looking pattern is a model for the shell of Olivia Poryphia, a.k.a. tent olive sea snail. Interestingly, this pattern can also be created via Elementary Cellular Automata. In fact, I've done this in a different project, see my paper-toaster repo.

The paper linked above describes a reaction-diffusion system in one dimension for how activator and inhibitor hormones move across the shell's edge. The shell is growing only at the edge, everything else is just a history of past growth, similar to a slit scan animation.

With this slit-scan interpretation in mind, we can examine the motion of the darker pigment:

  • The tips of triangles pointing up are events where the chemical reactions produce new pigment.
  • The sides of triangles pointing upwards show the pigment spreading out horizontally.
  • The sides of triangles pointing downwards represent two waves of pigment heading towards each other.
  • The tips of triangles pointing downwards are events where pigments collide. The chemical reaction causes these to cancel out and you get the lighter color afterwards.
