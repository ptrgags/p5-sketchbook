Conformal Geometric Algebra (CGA) Test (2026-02/2026-03)

Click/tap the canvas to cycle through the different scenes.

I'm porting the conformal geometry math I used in math-notebook to p5js so I can animate them. This time, I'm using 2D Conformal Geometric Algebra (2D CGA) rather than complex numbers.

Description of the scenes

  1. Transformation Basics - This first scene creates various shapes and transforms them to make sure the transformations look correct. The original shapes are in yellow. The blue shapes are the originals reflected through the yellow line. The small orange shapes are the originals inverted into the yellow circle.
  2. Conformal Transformation Basics - This second scene explores the unusual transformations possible through CGA.

    The big green circle represents the unit circle.

    The orange circles and the ring of green points near the center are moving under parabolic motions (related to translation). This was a precursor to my Parabolic Grid animation.

    The green points that hover around the left end of the unit circle are transformed by an elliptic motion. This is related to rotation.

    The green points moving across the unit circle from one end to the other are undergoing hyperbolic motion. This is related to scaling.

    The purple points are what happens when you combine the elliptic and hyperbolic motion, i.e. a loxodromic motion. This is related to moving along a logarithmic spiral.

  3. Animated Fractals - This scene shows two copies of the Sierpiński Triangle fractal, animated in different ways. The orange circles show how the fractal is created by overlaying scaled copies of the shape over and over. The green circles highlight the order that the code traverses the fractal when rendering.
  4. Nacho Spaceship - I added circular arcs that can be transformed by these conformal transformations. This animation tests them out. I take a nacho chip shape and rotate it to infinity... and beyond.
  5. {7, 3} Hyperbolic Tiling - This is a prototype of rendering hyperbolic tilings like this heptagonal tiling. This continues past explorations of this math from my math-notebook repo. This time, I figured out a better algorithm for traversing the tiling.
