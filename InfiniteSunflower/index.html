Infinite Sunflower (2024)

Recently I've found a few different sources that all mention the mathematical pattern of spiral phyllotaxis, the spiral arrangement of leaves/petals/primordia of various plants.

  • The Beauty of Numbers in Nature by Ian Stewart
  • The Algorithmic Beauty of Plants, Chapter 4 by Przemyslaw Prusinkiewicz and Aristid Lindenmayer
  • "Towards solving the mystery of spiral phyllotaxis" by Boris Rozin

The last article is most relevant here, as it talks not just about how the spirals relate to the Fibonacci sequence, but how the pattern develops as the inflorescence grows. It also links to animations of the growth pattern.

After reading that, I first explored the math of these spirals in Desmos and made this interactive graph.

Then, I wanted to do something more artistic, which resulted in this sketch. I create primordia in the center of the circle, and each moves radially outwards, each spaced a golden angle around the circle compared to the previous one. This is enough to produce the spiral pattern.

I'm using plenty of artistic license here. A real sunflower stops growing at a certain point, while my animation goes on forever. Also, on a real flower, a seed wouldn't turn into a petal like they do here.
