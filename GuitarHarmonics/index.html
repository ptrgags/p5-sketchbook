Guitar Harmonics (2026-05)

This sketch creates two diagrams about guitar harmonics.

The first diagram helps to locate harmonics along the guitar strings. The first several harmonics are located on (or very nearly on) a fret on the fretboard. However, higher harmonics tend to show up between frets in general.

To play a harmonic on guitar, lightly touch a string at the specified point, without pressing it to the fretboard. When you strum, this restricts which vibrations are possible. At the locations marked in the diagram, you should hear the harmonic ring out.

The second diagram illustrates what happens when you continue the pattern to higher and higher harmonics. You get a harmonic for every rational number between 0 and 1 (representing a fraction of the length of the string). In practice, this means there are harmonics everywhere along the string, arranged in this fractal pattern. However, higher harmonic numbers tend to produce more dissonant pitches.
