Differential Growth (2021, 2025)

This sketch is a simulation of differential growth. This is a pattern of organic growth you find in everything from plants to the brain. When the boundary of an organism grows rapidly, the internal forces cause it to fold up on itself to better fit the surrounding space.

Each "organism" is represented here by a polygon. The organism's boundary has many "boundary cells", represented as the vertices of the polygon. Each cell can move around independently, subject to several rules:

  • If cells get too close together, they experience a repulsion force. This both helps the organism avoid intersecting itself, as well as other organisms it encounters.
  • cells pull on their 2 connected neighbors. If a cell strays from its neighbors, it will experience tension that pulls it back like a spring.
  • Periodically, a new cell is grown at a random spot on the organism's boundary
  • If an edge of the polygon becomes too long, a new cell is grown at the midpoint, as if easing the strain on the organism
  • If the angle between adjacent edges becomes too small, a new cell is grown in an attempt to keep the curve round.
  • In this particular simulation, I clamp the vertices so the shape doesn't go off-screen.

This implementation still isn't quite right. For example, once space becomes limited, the curves start self-intersecting. I'm not sure how to detect this, let alone how to avoid it.

My inspiration for this sketch came from a few sources:

  • Exploring 2D differential growth with Javascript by Jason Webb
  • Differential Line and other related articles from Inconvergent
  • The Autonomous Agents chapter of The Nature of Code by Daniel Shiffman

This sketch was started in 2021 (see the logbook for the full history). I stopped after a while since I wasn't happy with the results. I returned to it in 2025 and cleaned it up to make what you see above. I used B-splines to smooth out the polylines into curves, which greatly improves the look. I also adjusted the simulation so the curves grow faster.
