Origami Tiles (2021)

This sketch makes a continuous pattern from connecting many triangular tiles. If this pattern were printed out (double-sided) and folded on the light grey lines in either direction, no matter how you fold it, it produces a seamless tiling!

To make this happen, I designed the tiles carefully so they have exactly the same pattern of dark and light parts on the boundary. For example, compare the following two tiles and see how the edges look the same even though the interior is very different.

Also notice how whever the curves meet the boundary of the triangle, the curve and the triangle edge are always perpendicular. This is the easiest way to make sure the curve doesn't suddenly bend when it moves into the neighboring tile.

For the mathematically inclined, this means the patterns are C1 continuous, not just C0 continuous.
