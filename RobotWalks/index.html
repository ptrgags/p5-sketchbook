Robot Walks (2025)

Use left/right arrows (or touch the left/right sides of the canvas) to move the robot.

This sketch is an interactive version of the printable artworks I made in my paper-toaster project. The Paper Toaster sketch is in turn inspired by Project Euler #208: Robot Walks. The goal is not to solve the original math puzzle about counting the possible paths, but just to visualize the patterns.

The robot moves in arcs that are 1/5 of a circle. This angle does not create a regular grid like 1/4 or 1/6 turns would. Depending on the robot's path, sometimes you get satisfying squiggly patterns, other times you get a chaotic pile of overlapping arcs. If you're careful, you can get the robot to make an intricate loop, or patterns with 5-fold rotation symmetry like a flower.
