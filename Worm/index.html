Worm (2025)

Move the mouse (desktop) or touch and drag the canvas (mobile) to move the worm around.

A while ago, I came across a fun animation technique from This YouTube video by user argonaut. You start with a chain of joints. There are simple constraints on how to update the positions, starting on one end of the chain and working towards the other. While not a realistic simulation, this method is relatively simple to implement and has a fun cartoon creature vibe. There are two main constraints in use here:

  1. Each joint follows a fixed distance behind the previous one. This causes the head of the worm to drag along the rest of the body.
  2. The angle between any 3 consecutive joints must be at least a minimum size. This prevents the curve from getting too pinched, and also creates S-bends like a snake.

I didn't implement any self-collision detection, so the worm can go through itself. This is not realistic, but it's amusing to create loops and watch as they unwind once they reach the tail.
