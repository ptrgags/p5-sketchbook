Growing Flowers (2023)

Every time the page is refreshed, a new plant is grown.

I made this as a generative art experiment that's fun to watch. At each time step, pick a node in the tree. A random neighbor is chosen (with a chance of skipping) to grow a new branch. If no new branches are added, the node becomes a "flower" and blooms after a delay.

This is similar to a random depth-first-search (DFS) maze generation algorithm, however:

  1. Growth can go upwards and sideways, but not downwards. Furthermore, upwards growth is weighted slightly higher.
  2. Every several simulation steps, I shuffle the list of unvisited nodes so the plant grows more "bushy". a true DFS produces a plant that mostly follows a single path.
