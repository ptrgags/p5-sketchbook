Parabolic MÃ¶bius transformations are the generalization of translations in conformal geometry. There's a single pole, acting as both a source and a sink. Points are repelled from one side, move along generalized circles and are attracted to the other side.

If you've ever seen diagrams of the field lines around an ideal electric dipole, it's quite similar, though here the motion is along perfect circles.

In the animation above, I make use of two parabolic transformations. The motion of the red circles is analagous to translation in the x-direction. The motion of the blue circles is analagous to translation along the y-direction.

Why is this related to translation? Consider a regular translation in the x-direction. It moves points away from infinity on the left, and towards infinity on the right. In conformal geometry, there's no +/- infinity, just a single point at infinity (See e.g. a description of the Riemann Sphere from complex analysis). So this point at infinity is acting both as a source and a sink. It's the same thing, it just looks different in the projected view.
