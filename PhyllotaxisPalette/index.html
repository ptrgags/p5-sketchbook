Phyllotaxis Palette (2024)

While thinking about the math of sunflower spirals (see the Infinite Sunflower sketch), I realized a possible use for this pattern. If the spiral is superimposed on the HSV color wheel (with value = 1), then every point corresponds to a unique color! And each successive color will be a different hue compared with the previous one.

However, that produces a washed-out color palette since there are many small points near the origin (white). Applying a square root to the saturation compensates for this.

This palette is but one of many possibilities. HSV is a 3D color space, and the points trace out a 1D spiral, so it's only possible to pick out a small sampling of colors at a time. Since I usually use a dark background, I kept the value at a maximum for good contrast. However, in other situations, a palette that tends towards grey or black might be more helpful.
